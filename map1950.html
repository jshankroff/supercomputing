1950
Washington, D.C.
President Truman signs the bill creating the National Science Foundation (NSF) in response to the report entitled "Science - the Endless Frontier" written by Vannevar Bush, head of the Office of Scientific Research and Development in July 1945.
1957
USSR launches the first satellite into orbit, Sputnik.
President Eisenhower immediately orders the establishment of the Departent of Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA).
1958
The National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) is established and takes over missile research from ARPA, leaving ARPA's sole mission as information technology research.
NSF funding greatly increases and their astronomy program forges a close working relationship with NASA.
